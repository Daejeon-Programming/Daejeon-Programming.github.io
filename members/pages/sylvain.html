Who am I ?

I'm a french mechanical engineer (or at least I graduated as one), after my Masters degree I worked for one year as a patent engineer in Paris. Because I was a bit bored and felt like that was a good time for me to try to "follow my dream", I decided to leave my comfort and to take one year off to study programming by myself. To make the whole experience even more interesting, I decided to go to live in Korea during this year (I already knew Korea from a year as an exchange student).
For this year of studying, I had (and still have somehow) the project to develop a 'language exchange app' for smartphone. This idea was also a good reason to live in a foreign country.

What were my programming experience at that point ?

Before last September (2016), I had little experience with programming, but never anything very serious. I took some of the opportunity, during high school and college to participate in school projects that involved programming, but I never gave any priority to studying this.
So during these projects I learned basic html and css, I tried learning C a few time, but always stopped in the middle because of a lack of motivating goals. In university I had a big project where the theme my group picked was "Develop an Android app." For for this project I learned how to use an Arduino board. And that's all I knew at that point.

What did I learn since ?

The fist thing I learned once I arrived in Korea (after I realized that my project might be a big mistake) is Java, because as far as I knew it was the main language use for Android apps... And it seems like a good beginning point. Once I felt a bit more confident with it, I began to take a look at how Android works... And I got a bit lost. After a while, I took the decision that maybe I should rework on my html/css knowledge and ended up feeling like web design and a web app would be way easier for a beginning... I learnt JavaScript and PHP, then I had a few small projects with it. Around Christmas I visited my friends and family in France, one of my friends told me that he was working in a startup in Canada, using node.js for his app. I told him that maybe I could help him. And so I began to learn node.js. Nowadays I'm finally able to help him with his app by writing some tests. I haven't continued my 'language exchange app' project recently, but still think about it... Recently I was thinking that I should probably find some people to build a project, that's why David's idea to have some project together motivate me so much.

A bit more detail on the 'language exchange app'

This idea came to me as I was learning Korean, when chatting with some Korean friends, sometime, I was trying to speak Korean with them. I knew that my Korean was full of mistakes and weirdly constructed sentences, but they would almost never correct these. I don't think that it's because they didn't want to help or just had no time to do this...
My analysis is that 'normal' messenger apps are not suitable for multilingual conversation because it's hard and annoying to keep a conversation alive and to correct mistakes or give advice about a language at the same time.
